## 1) Strategy Extracted from Internal Document

Core positioning in the provided ad concept:

- Investor entry product in Berlin with lower ticket ("small entry price")
- Tenant already in place, income from day one
- Framing as "wealth accumulation strategy" (long-term, patient investor)
- Focus on stability and reduced vacancy risk
- Suggestion to use portfolio angle (multiple units in same building)

Psychological framing recommended in the doc:

- Prefer: "discounted entry", "wealth accumulation", "investor opportunity", "secure tenant", "income from day one"
- Avoid legal-risk wording ("tenant protection problem", "eviction restriction", etc.)

## 2) Validated Market Facts (Trusted Sources)

## 2.1 Structural Housing Demand in Berlin

From Berlin Senate (StEP Wohnen 2040):

- Berlin identifies need for **222,000 additional units by 2040**
- Additional land reserve identified for **50,000 further units**
- 24 new major city quarters are part of the plan

Implication for campaign messaging:

- Demand/supply pressure is a defensible long-term narrative
- Supports "long-term wealth accumulation" positioning if phrased responsibly

## 2.2 Rent and Price Dynamics

From IBB Wohnungsmarktbericht 2024:

- Median asking rent in Berlin (2024): **15.74 EUR/m2** (+1.75 EUR/m2 YoY)
- Asking condo prices: **5,690 EUR/m2** in 2024 (approx. -1.0% YoY)
- Build activity softened (permits/completions pressure), while market remains tight

Implication:

- Storyline "rents remain strong while buy prices stabilized/corrected" is usable
- Should not promise fixed return, but can support "income-focused entry strategy"

## 2.3 Legal Context for Tenant-Occupied Units

From `BGB §577a` + Berlin tenant guidance:

- In conversion cases, purchaser self-use/economic termination rights can be blocked for long periods
- Berlin has a **10-year** protection framework via state regulation (as referenced by Berlin Senate tenant guide)

From `BGB §558`:

- Rent increase to local comparative rent requires legal conditions
- Rent must generally be unchanged for at least **15 months**
- Capping rule applies over 3 years (general 20%, lower thresholds possible in designated markets)

Implication:

- "Automatic increase every 3 years" is too absolute and should be avoided
- Better: "rent adjustments are legally regulated and possible under German law conditions"

## 3) Claims from Internal Doc - Risk/Validation Status

## 3.1 "45% cheaper than vacant apartment"

Status: **Not validated** from official market-wide source.

Recommendation:

- Do not publish as a general claim.
- If used, must be tied to specific unit-level comp evidence ("this unit vs comparable vacant listings"), with date and method.

## 3.2 "Steady increase in rental income every 3 years"

Status: **Overstated** / legally sensitive.

Recommendation:

- Replace with regulated wording:
  - "Potential rent adjustments are governed by German tenancy law and local market rules."

## 3.3 "Income from day one"

Status: **Conditionally valid** if tenant and lease are active at transfer date.

Recommendation:

- Keep with qualification:
  - "Tenant in place at closing (subject to contract and due diligence)."

## 4) Messaging Direction Supported by Evidence

Strong, defensible narrative:

- Berlin remains structurally undersupplied
- Tenant-occupied acquisition can reduce vacancy exposure
- Entry pricing can be more accessible vs certain vacant alternatives
- Strategy fits long-term, cashflow-oriented investors

Safe language examples:

- "Discounted entry potential vs selected vacant comparables"
- "Tenant in place at transfer"
- "Cashflow-oriented Berlin entry strategy"
- "Long-term hold in a supply-constrained market"

Language to avoid in ad/LP:

- Guaranteed returns
- Guaranteed rent increase cadence
- Broad % discount claims without auditable comp set
- Any wording implying legal outcomes are certain

## 5) Recommended Data/Disclosure Blocks for the Landing Page

For compliance and conversion quality:

1. **Investor Snapshot Box**
   - Current rent (monthly/annual)
   - Current occupancy status
   - Service costs / non-recoverables (if available)
   - Purchase price and simple gross yield range

2. **Legal Notes (Plain Language)**
   - Tenant rights framework in Germany/Berlin
   - Rent adjustment subject to legal requirements
   - Due diligence is mandatory

3. **Comparable Method Statement**
   - If "discounted entry" is claimed:
   - show comp basis (source, radius, size filter, date range)

4. **Risk Disclosure**
   - Vacancy, capex, legal/regulatory, financing, macro-rate risk

Most aligned audiences:
